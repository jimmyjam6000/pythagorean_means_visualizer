Pythagorean Means (upper semicircle, radius = 1)

a + b = 2 ⇒ AM = 1
Geometric-mean construction: split the diameter into lengths a and b. The perpendicular from the split point up to the semicircle has length GM = √(ab), and it always stays inside the semicircle.

Here we enforce a + b = 2 so the arithmetic mean is always 1. The harmonic mean becomes HM = 2ab/(a+b) = ab. The quadratic mean can exceed 1 (that’s normal): QM ≥ AM.
